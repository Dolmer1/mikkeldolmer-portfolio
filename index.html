Master's thesis

For my Master's thesis I developed Gut-Instincts, an ensemble-based biomedical NLP system for the GutBrainIE challenge at CLEF 2025, focused on named-entity recognition and relation extraction from gut-brain axis research papers. The project combined multiple transformer-based biomedical language models with advanced techniques such as CRF layers, BiLSTM architectures, ensemble voting, and negative sampling to improve robustness on noisy scientific data.

The system achieved first place in all three relation extraction subtasks and second place in the named-entity recognition task.
